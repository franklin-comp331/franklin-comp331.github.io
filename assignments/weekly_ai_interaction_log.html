Weekly AI Interaction Log

Course Name/Number: COMP 331: AI-Assisted Software Engineering
Weeks: 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10
Points: 10 points each week / 90 total

1 Purpose of Assignment

This assignment is intended to help you learn to do the following:

  • Develop intentional and structured use of GitHub Copilot or another approved AI/LLM tool
  • Reflect on how prompting, iteration, and evaluation affect outcomes
  • Identify patterns in successful and unsuccessful AI interactions
  • Build awareness of your own engineering decision-making process
  • Document how AI-assisted development evolves over time

2 Steps / Requirements of Assignment

Submit a separate AI interaction reflection for Weeks 2 through 10. This is the same recurring assignment, but every submission should cover only the current course week. Focus on the AI-assisted work, lab activity, reading, debugging, testing, or project development completed during the week.

Create a new document for every submission. Do not submit one cumulative document containing multiple weeks unless your professor explicitly asks for it. Submit the reflection through Canvas as a document or PDF, such as .docx or .pdf, unless your professor approves another format.

Your entry should include:

  1. Week number and date
  2. Development task or learning activity
  3. Your intent before using Copilot
  4. Copilot mode used: inline suggestion, chat, edit, agent/workspace, test generation, review, or debugging
  5. Prompt, comment, or interaction summary
  6. AI tool output or suggestion summary
  7. What you accepted, modified, or rejected
  8. Verification method: test, compiler, manual review, comparison, or rejected output
  9. Reflection on what worked, what failed, and what you will change next time

Suggested template:

Intent:
Copilot mode used:
Context I gave Copilot:
Copilot output:
What I accepted:
What I changed:
How I verified it:
What I learned:

3 Action Items

  • Click the link to the full hosted assignment instructions: Weekly AI Interaction Log.
  • Follow the instructions in the assignment page.
  • Use these same instructions for every log submission from Week 2 through Week 10.
  • Go to your preferred document editor, such as Google Docs at docs.google.com, Microsoft Word, or another professor-approved format.
  • Create a new document for the current log only. Do not include previous weeks.
  • Document meaningful GitHub Copilot interactions from the current week’s work, including prompts, outputs, accepted/rejected suggestions, and verification notes.
  • Write the required reflection paragraph about your initial prompt, prompt iterations, successful prompt patterns, unsuccessful prompt patterns, and what you will change next time.
  • Export or save the current week’s entry as a document or PDF and submit it through the Canvas submission item for the current course week.

4 Deliverables

Submit only the current week’s reflection as a separate document or PDF through Canvas. The submitted file should contain that week’s log entry only, not a cumulative log from multiple weeks. A repository link is not required for this weekly reflection unless your professor specifically asks for one. Do not rely on lab repository notes as your weekly submission unless your professor explicitly approves that format.

Suggested filename format:

week02-ai-reflection-yourname.pdf

5 Grading

The log submission is worth 10 points.

Category Points
Clear task context and intent 2
Copilot mode and interaction documented with enough specificity 3
Verification and human decision-making explained 3
Reflection identifies a useful pattern, risk, or next step 2
Weekly Total 10

Across Weeks 2-10, the assignment is worth 90 points total.

6 Submission Instructions

Submit the current week’s reflection document or PDF through Canvas. The file should contain only that week’s log entry. Follow any additional instructions provided by your professor.
